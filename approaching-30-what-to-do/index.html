How to fight against the fact that you're going to turn 30

By Donald Hutchison July 29, 2016 Comment

Turning 29 has made me realise that the task of getting onto the Forbes “30 under 30” list is now on hard mode. So I guess I’d better use this sinking feeling of fleeting youth to motivate me to finish some projects.

Additionally, when I was 18 I hired an assassin to murder me before I become 30, so that I would never live long enough to surrender my childish views. This makes everyday I do not get killed a thrilling occurrance and each passing moment seems precious and wonderful. I highly recommend this.

How to ignore the inevitable

Learn Guitar (again)

I have a ton of Total Guitar magazines from around 2003. These have followed me as I moved from apartment to apartment and even across countries. They have remained untouched for many years.

By the time I turn 30 I will have owned these magazines for longer than I have not owned them. The idea that I have been carrying around these symbols of “I want to do this, but I’ll get round to it later” like a personal albatross of failure for more than half my life is distressing to say the least.

Goal : Learn to play the songs that don’t suck (at least 5) and upload stupid cover versions to youtube

Release an indie game

I have been involved in the indie gaming scene on and off for close to 8 years. Shitposting on tigsource before that was even a thing.

There are a ton of projects that I’ve started and abandoned with an incredible power of abandonment. Actually finishing something would give me a great sense of personal achievement, and let me know it’s possible to actually complete tasks instead of letting them drop down the back of the couch over time.

Goal: Get a game on the app store and convince at least one person to download it.

Run better

In 2014 I decided to run a marathon because I wanted to prove that there’s nothing special about it (Also I found a link to The Non Runner’s Marathon Trainer on hacker news). If you commit to a training plan and do it, it’s pretty much impossible to be unable to finish the marathon.

The training took 3 months and it was more than slightly involving. But at the end of it I was able to run the whole cursed 42.2km round the west end of Berlin.

It’s kind of a metaphor for something, I don’t know what. I guess drawing, programming, weightlifting, music and anything else are pretty similiar in that if you just spend a ton of time doing it, it’s impossible not to achieve some level of proficiency. There’s no real “oh you’re so talented you must have been born that way I could never do that blablabla” factor in these things. If you were locked in a room with nothing but a piano and a sofa for 40 years, you’d eventually become pretty good at something that resembles piano music.

Anyway, when running I was always aggressively slow - overly concerned about not being able to run the distance if I went too fast. Now I’d like to set the pretty modest goal of running 10k in less than an hour. It’s not outrageous but would require some kind of effort.

Goal: 10k in an hour

Electronics

I studied Electronic and Electrical Engineering at university for a year before I switched to software, so I’ve always had a kind of dabbling in these things. However it was only quite recently that I finally discovered the world of arduinos and how small microchip programs open the way to interacting with the real world, with a minimal spaghetti mess of analog components and bad math (my fault).

There are a few things I want to create before I can consider this itch scratched.

  • some kind of midi to cv converter
  • the arduinoboy - (send midi signals to a gameboy)
  • The classic smokeyamp
  • A device to detect when my mailbox is opened, and thereby launch my apartment into the IoT

Goal: do all the above things and blog about it

Misc

  • I’ve been working on some cool things for Unity test automation and scripting Android devices. I’d like to tidy these up and open source them so that the world can benefit from my terrible code.
  • I have a ton of musical equipment, but I’ve been feeling too burnt out recently to do anything with it. I want to make at least one cheesy 80’s synthwave jam before I hit 30 and my life ends.
